Ingredients:

For the Meat Base (Korma)

  • Meat: 1 kg (Beef or Mutton, preferably boneless chunks)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Spices: 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, and 2 tsp Garam Masala or Haleem Masala.
  • Yogurt: 1 cup (whisked)
  • Oil/Ghee: 1 cup
  • Salt: To taste

For the Grains and Lentils

  • Wheat (Broken/Dalia): 1.5 cups (soaked overnight)
  • Barley: 1/2 cup (soaked overnight)
  • Lentil Mix: 1/4 cup each of Moong, Masoor, Maash (Urad), and Chana dal (soaked for 2 hours)
  • Water: 8-10 cups for boiling

For Garnish and Tempering (Tarka)

  • Onions: 2 large (thinly sliced and fried until golden)
  • Fresh Herbs: Chopped mint, coriander, and julienned ginger.
  • Green Chilies: 3-4 (finely chopped)
  • Lemon: 2-3 wedges
  • Chaati Masala: For sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Grains

    In a large pot, add the soaked wheat, barley, and mixed lentils with plenty of water and a pinch of turmeric. Boil on medium heat until completely soft and mushy. Once cooked, blend them slightly with a hand blender or a wooden masher (ghotna) to reach a coarse paste consistency.

  2. Prepare the Meat Korma

    In a pressure cooker or heavy pot, heat oil/ghee and sauté the meat with ginger-garlic paste. Add the red chili powder, turmeric, salt, and Haleem masala. Stir in the yogurt and cook until the oil separates. Add water and pressure cook until the meat is "fall-apart" tender.

  3. Shred the Meat

    Remove the cooked meat from the gravy. Shred the meat finely (discarding any bones) using a fork or a masher. Keep the leftover gravy (shorba) aside.

  4. Combine and Mash (Ghotna)

    Add the shredded meat and the leftover gravy into the pot of blended grains. This is the most important step: use a heavy wooden masher to vigorously mix and mash the mixture over low heat. This "ghotna" process creates the signature stretchy, gluey texture of Haleem.

  5. Final Simmer and Tempering

    Let the mixture simmer on low flame for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. In a separate pan, heat a little ghee and fry some sliced onions until crispy, then pour this (tarka) over the Haleem.

  6. Serving

    Dish out the Haleem into bowls. Top with the fried onions, fresh ginger, mint, coriander, chopped green chilies, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Sprinkle chaat masala for extra zest.

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade

  • Chicken: 1 kg (large bone-in pieces are best for flavor)
  • Yogurt: 1 cup (whisked)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Spices: 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp cumin powder, and 2 tsp Biryani Masala or Garam Masala.
  • Acid: Juice of 1 lemon.
  • Salt: To taste.

For the Rice

  • Basmati Rice: 3 cups (long-grain, soaked for at least 30 minutes)
  • Whole Spices: 2-3 green cardamoms, 1 black cardamom, 2 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, and 2 bay leaves.
  • Water: Plenty of water for boiling.
  • Salt: Use enough salt so the water "tastes like the sea."

For Biryani Base and Layering

  • Onions: 3 large (thinly sliced and fried until golden brown—Birista)
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium (finely chopped)
  • Fresh Herbs: 1 cup of fresh mint leaves and 1 cup of coriander (cilantro), chopped.
  • Green Chilies: 4-5 (slit lengthwise)
  • Fats: ½ cup oil or ghee.
  • Aromatics: A pinch of Saffron soaked in warm milk (or yellow food coloring) and 1 teaspoon of Kewra water or Rosewater for that signature restaurant aroma.

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken

    In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, lemon juice, and salt. Let this rest for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the fridge for the best flavor).

  2. Prepare the Fried Onions

    Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot (a "handi" or Dutch oven). Fry the sliced onions on medium heat until they are deep golden brown and crispy.
    Important: Remove half of the onions and set them aside on a paper towel for layering later.

  3. Cook the Chicken Base

    In the same pot with the remaining onions and oil, add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Sauté until the tomatoes soften. Add the marinated chicken and cook on high heat for 5 minutes. Lower the heat, cover, and cook for 15 minutes until the chicken is 80% cooked and the gravy is thick.

  4. Parboil the Rice

    Bring a large pot of water to a boil with whole spices and salt. Add soaked Basmati rice and boil for 5–7 minutes. Drain the rice when it is long and grainy but still has a firm "snap" when broken; it should not be fully soft.

  5. Layer the Biryani

    Spread the parboiled rice evenly over the chicken base in the pot. Top with the reserved fried onions, chopped mint, coriander, and saffron-infused milk. Drizzle a tablespoon of ghee and kewra water over the surface.

  6. The Dum (Steaming)

    Seal the pot tightly with a lid or foil to trap the steam. Cook on very low heat for 15–20 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest unopened for 10 minutes before gently fluffing the rice with a fork to serve.

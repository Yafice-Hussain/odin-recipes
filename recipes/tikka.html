Ingredients:

For the Chicken & Marinade

  • Chicken: 800g (boneless thighs or breast, cut into 2-inch cubes)
  • Yogurt: 1/2 cup (thick or Greek yogurt)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1.5 tablespoons
  • Spices: 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder, 1 tsp cumin powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, and 1 tsp Garam Masala.
  • Aromatics: 1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves (Kasuri Methi), crushed.
  • Acid: 2 tablespoons lemon juice.
  • Fats: 2 tablespoons mustard oil (for authentic smoky flavor) or vegetable oil.
  • Salt: To taste.

For Basting & Cooking

  • Butter: 2-3 tablespoons (melted, for brushing)
  • Skewers: Metal or bamboo (soak bamboo skewers in water for 30 minutes).

For Serving

  • Onion Rings: 1 small onion, sliced.
  • Fresh Herbs: Fresh coriander for garnish.
  • Chutney: Mint-yogurt chutney.
  • Lemon: Extra wedges for squeezing.

Instructions:

  1. First Marination

    In a large bowl, toss the chicken cubes with lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt. Let this sit for 15–20 minutes. This helps the meat stay juicy and absorb the spices deeper.

  2. Second Marination

    To the same bowl, add the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, remaining spices, mustard oil, and crushed Kasuri Methi. Mix thoroughly until every piece is well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (ideally overnight).

  3. Skewering

    Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the skewers. Leave a tiny bit of space between the pieces so the heat can circulate and cook the edges evenly.

  4. Grilling / Oven Method

    Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Place the skewers on a baking rack over a tray. Bake for 15–20 minutes, turning halfway through. Pro-Tip: Use the "Broil" or top grill setting for the last 2-3 minutes to get those charred, blackened edges.

  5. Basting

    Halfway through the cooking process, brush the chicken generously with melted butter. This prevents the chicken from drying out and gives it a professional restaurant-style glaze.

  6. The Charcoal Smoke (Optional)

    For a true "Tandoori" flavor, place a small piece of burning charcoal in a steel bowl in the middle of your cooked chicken container. Drizzle a drop of oil on the coal and seal the lid for 2 minutes to infuse the meat with smoke.
